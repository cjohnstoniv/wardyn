## How the score works

`score = min(raw, ceiling)` per dimension; composite = weighted mean (security and
reliability count double; Σw = 14).

- **raw** = `(pass + 0.5·partial) / (pass + partial + fail)` over that dimension's
  criteria verdicts.
- **ceiling** = the worst OPEN finding above low-confidence in that dimension:
  crit→35, high→60, med→85, none→100.
- Any open high-confidence HIGH clamps the whole composite to ≤74.

The composite is **86** and therefore un-clamped: every high-confidence HIGH is
verified-resolved (the gate is lifted). Eight of twelve dimensions land at 85,
ceiling-bound (raw sits higher, but the worst *open* finding in each is a MED, so
the ceiling clamps to 85) — 85 is their honest cap without clearing every open
MED. CLI-UX is clean at 100. Three (documentation 83, supply chain 83,
build/release/CI 83) are raw-bound below their ceiling. (8 + 1 + 3 = 12.)

## What makes a claim count (unchanged across all three rounds)

- **Resolution is machine-verified.** A finding is credited only when its
  `resolved:{sha,test}` re-proves on every scorer run: the commit must be an
  ancestor of the scored ref *and* the named test must still exist in the tree. An
  unbacked marker is silently ignored — it can under-count, never fake.
- **Verdicts move only by blind re-assessment.** Reviewers read the current code
  with no prior verdicts and no target score. Hand-editing a verdict is forbidden.
  Round 3's numbers come from **26 blind lane re-assessments** across three waves
  (7 architecture/maintainability lanes, 15 security/reliability/UX lanes at full
  coverage, then 4 re-judged after the fixes below).
- **Fixes carry counterfactual tests.** Each non-trivial fix was verified by a
  test that fails if the fix is reverted (e.g. removing the orchestrator's
  restore-from-store block fails the multi-substrate kill-switch recovery test;
  removing the docker `register.go` init fails the substrate-registry resolve).

## The honest dip, and why the final number is trustworthy

The middle of round 3 is the most important part of the record. After the ten
groups above landed, a **comprehensive 15-lane blind re-assessment at full
coverage** — security and reliability judged against the crown review's expanded
17-criterion checklists — did two things at once:

1. It moved Security and Reliability **82 → 85** (the full-coverage pass credited
   the fixes the earlier structural-only pass could not see), and surfaced **zero
   new CRIT or HIGH** findings across the whole codebase.
2. Being stricter than the prior round on the user-facing lanes, it **dropped
   CLI-UX 100 → 80 and DX/onboarding 85 → 78**, and surfaced ~24 real LOW defects
   — including a **doubled `wardyn: wardyn:` error prefix that this round's own SDK
   migration had introduced**.

The composite dipped to 84. Rather than merge the friendlier earlier verdicts and
score around it, round 3 **fixed the real defects the adversarial pass found**
(the S3 commits) and then re-judged the affected lanes on the fixed tree. That
re-judge — blind, no target — returned CLI-UX to 100 and DX to 85 and lifted
Build/CI to 85, for a final composite of **86**. The number is trustworthy
*because* it survived being driven down first.

## The coverage correction (S1 — no score changed)

Security read 55% coverage going into round 3, which invited the reading that half
its criteria were unevaluated. Investigation showed the 55% was a **denominator
artifact**: 8 security lanes × a shared 10-criterion checklist = 80 rows, of which
36 were correctly marked "not-evaluated — owned by lane X" (scope partitions). The
scorer now measures coverage over **unique criteria with an owning-lane verdict**,
and drops a stale hardcoded "capped" set for lanes the crown review has since
deep-reviewed. This is bookkeeping honesty, not score-gaming: it was verified by
diffing `scores.json` before and after — **every dimension's score/raw/ceiling and
the composite are byte-identical; only the printed coverage moved** (security
55→100, reliability 68→100, architecture 90→100). Coverage never enters the score.

## A note on who reviewed this round

The plan pinned Fable as the reviewer/finalizer for every diff and re-assessment.
Partway through round 3 the account switched and Fable became unavailable ("usage
credits are required for this model"). Per the standing rule that a reviewer is
**Opus or Fable, never the implementing tier**, every Fable call fell back to
**Opus**, and the fallback was recorded rather than papered over.

Then Fable came back online for a final validation pass, and it did exactly what a
pinned reviewer should: it **confirmed all four S3 fix commits** (spinning up a
live Postgres to prove migration 0022's concurrent-dedup under `-race`),
**independently re-judged the four recovery lanes blind**, and audited this
document against the scorer (SHIP, no discrepancy). Its lane re-judge was
**stricter than Opus's** — it held Build/release/CI at 83 rather than 85 — and
that stricter verdict is the one that stands here. Taking the pinned reviewer's
*less* favorable number over the fallback's is the whole point of the method. No
review was done by the model that wrote the code under review.

## Honest residuals (open, disclosed, not credited)

- **Helm cannot produce a working deploy.** No published `wardynd` image and no
  Kubernetes runner driver (both v0.5). The from-source **compose** path works and
  is CI-validated; the helm gap is disclosed in `deploy/helm` and holds the
  Build/CI "working deploy" criterion at partial. Together with the release-flag
  item below, it is why Build/release/CI is raw-bound at **83**, not 85.
- **The three GitHub releases are still flagged non-prerelease** while the project
  is pre-alpha. The one-command fix (`gh release edit <tag> --prerelease`) is an
  outward-facing action an automation safety classifier blocked the agent from
  running; it is left for an operator to execute. `RELEASING.md` discloses it, and
  Fable's re-judge correctly held the release-process criterion partial for it.
  Running it flips that criterion to pass and lifts Build/release/CI to 85.
- **~17 LOW findings remain** from the comprehensive pass (stale threat-model
  prose in a couple more spots, JVM/Deno trust-store gaps now *documented* but not
  closed — closing them needs per-runtime CA-install work in the agent images).
  None are above low confidence; none gate the score. The highest-value ones, plus
  the cheap doc sweeps (e.g. `audit <id>` positional form across docs/examples),
  were fixed this round.
- **Function-level size is now gated but not zero-residual.** `make lint` enforces
  funlen 150 / gocyclo 30 / gocognit 60 and is green; three functions carry a
  justified `//nolint:funlen`, and six 1000+-line files are justified in
  ARCHITECTURE.md behind frozen size caps rather than decomposed.
- **`GO-2026-5932`** (x/crypto/openpgp, unmaintained, no fix) is accepted: reached
  only via `filippo.io/age`, flagged subpackage never imported; `govulncheck`
  reports 0 called-path vulnerabilities on both tag sets.
